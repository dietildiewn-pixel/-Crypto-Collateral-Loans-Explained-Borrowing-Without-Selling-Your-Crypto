Crypto collateral loans explained. Learn how borrowing against Bitcoin or Ethereum works, how crypto loans use collateral, and why investors access liquidity without selling their digital assets.

---

## What Is a Crypto Collateral Loan?

A **crypto collateral loan** allows investors to borrow money using cryptocurrency as security.

Instead of selling digital assets like Bitcoin or Ethereum, borrowers deposit their crypto as **collateral** and receive a loan.

When the loan is repaid, the collateral is returned.

This allows investors to unlock liquidity while keeping their crypto holdings.

---

## How Crypto Collateral Loans Work

The process is usually straightforward.

### Step 1: Deposit Crypto

The borrower deposits cryptocurrency such as BTC or ETH into a secured wallet controlled by the lending platform.

---

### Step 2: Loan-to-Value Calculation

The platform calculates how much the borrower can receive based on the **loan-to-value (LTV)** ratio.

Example:

- Crypto collateral value: **$10,000**
- LTV: **50%**

Loan received: **$5,000**

---

### Step 3: Receive Loan Funds

The borrower receives funds in:

- USD
- USDC
- USDT
- other stablecoins

Funds can be used for any purpose.

---

### Step 4: Repay the Loan

Once the borrower repays the loan plus interest, the crypto collateral is returned.

---

## Advantages of Crypto Collateral Loans

Many investors prefer crypto loans because they:

- maintain exposure to crypto markets
- avoid selling long-term holdings
- provide quick liquidity
- require no credit checks

---

## Risks to Understand

Because crypto prices fluctuate, borrowers must monitor collateral value.

If the market drops sharply, the platform may require additional collateral or partial repayment.

This protects the lender against default risk.

---

## Final Thoughts

Crypto collateral loans provide a powerful alternative to selling digital assets.

By using cryptocurrency as collateral, investors can access liquidity while maintaining long-term exposure to the crypto market.
